Gabe Gator in: Gator Crossing Gallery

A small showcase of work I did for a solo game art project at UCF, Gabe Gator in: Gator Crossing.


Gabe Gator concept art

Some prelimiary sketches I created when trying to decide on the tone and style of my game art class project. I landed on an ironically meek and timid alligator character named Gabe who lives in the sewers of New York, and intentionally gave him cooler, less aggressive colors, softer points and a smaller stature to help convey his general demeanor.

Rat Pack concept art

Naturally with a hero character, the game needs enemies, which gave me the idea for rat hooligans that rule the sewers Gabe lives in. To align with the New York vibe, I also made a not-yet-used ranged variant based on a mafia member.

Rat Pack concept art

The assignment also called for a main antagonist, so I decided to give the aforementioned rat pack a proper leader. He's a very standard big bad for this style of platformer, but I'm proud of his design nonetheless.

Fun fact: the name "Big Cheese" was actually suggested by my mom when I was telling her about the assignment as I was working on it. Thank you, mom.

In-Game Assets

A small collection of in-game assets, including two animations of Gabe, an idle animation for Big Cheese, and the first (and so far only) level's tile set.

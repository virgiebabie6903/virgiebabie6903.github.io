Queso Caper! Gallery

A showcase of the 2D and 3D work I did for my UCF game production project, Queso Caper!

The prompt of the assignment was to make a 30-second game featuring a hallway with a guard. My group initially proposed the idea of Plankton stealing the formula from Mr. Krabs, but we then remembered that copyrighted content was probably not the way to go, so I quickly designed a new set of characters to give off the idea of a small protagonist getting past a big antagonist, while still keeping the initial cartoony premise in tact.


Main protagonist preliminary sketches

The most tried-and-true "small thief" type of character is a rat, so that's what I immediately tried for when designing the characters for this game. I went out of my way to make him look a bit goofy, though, as to not appear mean or antagonistic.

When my teammates asked me for a name, I named him Rupert on the spot for no particular reason.

Main antagonist preliminary sketch

To keep the initial concept in tact, we wanted Rupert to be infiltrating a kitchen, with this cleaver-wielding chef as its guard. His face is intentionally smushed and his hands are intentionally simplified, as we were on a time crunch through the whole semester and I didn't want to give ourselves more work than necessary.

I am responsible for his name too, Chef Kitzencinque (pronounced "Kitchen Sink". It's French, probably).

In-game models

For this project, I was also responsible for modeling, rigging and animating the main characters, as well as the collectible cheese. For the sake of time and consistency, I worked in Blender, and thankfully was able to adapt the models from the concept sketches smoothly. If our project were more ambitious, the models would likely look a bit cleaner, but for our cartoonish aesthetic and overall scope, these were perfectly fine for getting the job done.

Title, win and lose screens

This was my first time doing fully-rendered art for menu screens. In this one in particular, I tried to replicate the style of cartoon title cards (minus the Comic Sans font, that was just there because our team was running short on time and I didn't want to draw each individual letter).

These drawings of Rupert tried to be more faithful to his final model than the original sketches, which had more centered eyes and a better defined neck. I am particularly proud of how the shading on these pieces came out.
